Total Factor Productivity


Total Factor Productivity (TFP) represents growth in firm's output which is in excess of the growth of labor and capital inputs. TFP is a measure of the efficiency with which firms combine their labor and capital inputs when producing goods.

The scatter shows that, in general, municipalities with lowest TFPs in 1993 are the ones that grew most in the following 20 years. The Regression Analysis shows that the convergence rate is of 0.03 (~3.3% annually).
